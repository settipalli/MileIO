The story behind the creation of MileIO

The author of the MileIO project purchased his first car in the year 2009. Being a data centric person, he started to track every trip, fuel refil and maintenance expenses of his car by jotting them down in a dairy.

Eventually, he realized that it was tiresome and time consuming to track and draw conclusions from analyzing hand written details of the trips and vehicle expenses. This was the inspiration behind the thought of using software to solve the real world problem at hand.

Fast forward, the year is 2015. The author purchased his second car. This time around, he choose to use an app installed on his phone to track trips. The app he chose to use was MileIQ: https://www.mileiq.com/. The interface of the app is magnificant and it allowed automatic tracking of trips.

Though tracking trips automatically without any manual input was a boon, the author soon realized the cost of using such features. The battery drain due to the persistent use of GPS was something that could not be ignored. The solution was to have his mobile device connected to the car charger as long as he is driving.

The second problem was more of a pain in the neck and hard to resolve and eventually led the author to switch to a different miles tracking technique. This was the problem of false positives. Thought the app was exceptionally designed with a well thought out user interface, the automatic tracking was not accurate. If you had to stop for a considerable amount of time waiting for a traffic signal to turn green or drive at a snail pace due to congested roads, the app would consider it as a termination point for the on-going trip and terminate the trip. When you come across multiple such incidents on a single trip, the app would have either sub-divided the trip into multiple individual trips or would have missed tracking few miles in-between. This implied that the author had to consistently review the output of the app and update the trips as appropriate.

The problem described above caused the author to switch over to using Google Sheets to track not just the trip data but also fuel and maintenance expenses. There was no automatic tracking which meant that every trip and vehicle expense had to be manual added to a workbook (sheet) using the Google Sheets app. This was not a problem since the author had to anyway review and update every trip for accuracy when using app(s) that tracked trips automatically. Moreover, the app that the author was using did not support tracking fuel and other expenses.

The use of Google Sheets for tracking miles and vehicle expences worked beautifully for several months until the author sold his car. This experience showcased a huge opportunity to the author that eventually led to creation of MileIO that allows a user to track not just trips but also fuel and other maintenance expenses for multiple automobiles.

About MileIO

MileIO is a simplified mileage tracking and fleet management application. It cuts the hassle of all the paper work involved while tracking your miles and vehicle expenses manually. Though the concept of tracking mileage and fleet management is old and generic in nature, it would be unfair to not acknowledge the existing applications in the market that influenced the design of MileIO. Few of those are listed below:

  1. TripLog: https://triplogmileage.com/
  2. MileIQ: https://www.mileiq.com/
  3. Mileage Expense Log (iOS): http://www.chuchutrain.com/mileage-expense-log.html

Open Source

MileIO application is built utlizing the Python Flask microframework. It uses Google Sheets as a backend data store in a hope that the user always has access to his data stored in his Google Drive account regardless of whether he chooses to use the MileIO interface or not. The source code is licensed under the MIT License and is available at https://github.com/settipalli/mileio.

Features

  • Log every drive.
  • Log fuel and maintenance expenses for your vehicle.
  • Manage more than one automobile.
  • Categorize your drives as personal or business.
